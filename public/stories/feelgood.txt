🌞 The Day Joy Came Back
Rita woke up feeling a little sad.
She didn’t know why.
Nothing was wrong, but nothing felt right either.
She sat on her bed and looked around her small room.
Her books were neatly stacked.
Her clothes were folded.
Her window was open, and the morning air was fresh.
But her heart felt heavy.
“I hope today feels better,” she whispered to herself.

🌼 A Small Start
Rita walked to the kitchen.
Her grandmother was making tea.
The room smelled warm and sweet.
“Good morning, dear,” Grandma said.
Rita smiled weakly.
“Good morning.”
Grandma looked at her closely.
“You look a little down.”
Rita shrugged.
“Just one of those days.”
Grandma gently placed a hand on her shoulder.
“Then today, we will look for tiny joys.
They are hiding everywhere.”
Rita didn’t fully understand, but she nodded.

🐦 A Tiny Visitor
Later, Rita sat in the garden.
The sun felt soft, not too bright.
The flowers were swaying in the breeze.
Suddenly, a small blue bird landed near her feet.
It tilted its head.
It hopped closer.
It chirped a sweet little song.
Rita’s sadness lifted a little.
Just a tiny bit.
But she felt it.
“You’re cute,” she whispered.
The bird fluttered its wings and flew away.
But the soft joy it left stayed.

🍋 The Lemonade Stand
Rita decided to take a walk.
On the corner of the street, she saw two little kids setting up a lemonade stand.
They waved at her.
“Do you want lemonade?”
Rita smiled.
“Yes, please.”
One of the kids gave her a cup with both hands, carefully, as if it was something precious.
Rita took a sip.
It was sweet, cold, and perfect.
The kids watched her eagerly.
“It’s delicious,” she said.
Their faces lit up with the biggest smiles.
Rita felt her heart warm up again.

🌻 A Gift from a Stranger
As she continued walking, she passed an elderly man selling small plants.
One plant had a bright yellow flower—a tiny sunflower.
Rita stopped to look.
The old man smiled.
“You like sunflowers?”
Rita nodded.
“They look happy.”
He carefully picked up the pot and handed it to her.
“This one is for you.”
Rita blinked in surprise.
“Oh! I… I don’t have money right now.”
The old man shook his head kindly.
“Some flowers are meant to go to the right people.”
Rita felt her eyes shine.
“Thank you… truly.”
She held the pot close to her chest as she walked home.

☀️ Joy Returns
Back home, Rita placed the sunflower on her window.
The bright yellow petals glowed in the sunlight.
Her grandmother came in and smiled.
“That flower looks just like you,” she said.
“Like me?”
“Yes,” Grandma replied.
“Bright.
Warm.
And stronger than you think.”
Rita looked at the sunflower again.
She felt something she hadn’t felt in many days—
Lightness.
Warmth.
Joy.
Not a huge joy.
Not the loud kind.
But a soft, gentle, peaceful joy.
The kind that fills the heart slowly.
She whispered to herself,
“Today was better.”
And for the first time in a while…
She truly believed it.

🌈 The Feel-Good Message
Sometimes joy doesn’t come in big moments.
Sometimes it comes in:

a small bird
a sweet drink
a kind smile
a tiny flower
or a gentle word

Tiny joys are everywhere.
And when you let them in,
even a heavy day can become light again.
